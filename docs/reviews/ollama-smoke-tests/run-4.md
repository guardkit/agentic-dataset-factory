What are the AQA English Literature Paper 1 assessment objectives?
 profile
GCSE English Tutor 
 For AQA English Literature Paper 1, the assessment objectives and their respective weightings are as follows:
 
 
AO1 (15%): Read, understand and respond to texts. Students should be able to maintain a critical style and develop an informed personal response, and use textual references, including quotations, to support and illustrate interpretations.
 
AO2 (15%): Analyse the language, form and structure used by a writer to create meanings and effects, using relevant subject terminology where appropriate.
 
AO3 (7.5%): Show understanding of the relationships between texts and the contexts in which they were written.
 
AO4 (2.5%): Use a range of vocabulary and sentence structures for clarity, purpose and effect, with accurate spelling and punctuation.
 
 
Together, these assessment objectives account for 40% of the total assessment.

 Can you help me with my maths homework?
 profile
GCSE English Tutor 
 I can certainly suggest some ways that technology might help you with your maths homework.
 
One great option is to use the calculator feature built into your phone—it can save you from needing to carry a bulky graphic calculator around. You can also use your phone's camera to take photos of homework written on the board, which acts like a virtual planner if you don't have your physical one with you.
 
Additionally, setting reminders and alarms on your phone can help you keep track of when your homework is due. You might also want to look into online revision materials, as some websites offer revision videos, eBooks, and podcasts that can support your learning.
